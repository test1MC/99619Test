Survey

The is used to survey Azimuth, Dip and Roll values. There are two types of surveys:

  • Rig Alignment: The drill rig is aligned to achieve accurate azimuth and dip values based on planned values prior to drilling.
  • Collar Capture: The captures the Azimuth and Dip values of an existing drillhole. This is done at the collar of the drillhole.

Both surveys are executed the same way:

  1. The is mounted onto the rig
  2. The drill rig aligns or the captures the Azimuth and Dip values
  3. The tablet is used to monitor the alignment in real time
  4. The survey is saved as either Rig Alignment or Collar Capture

The survey aligns or captures the Azimuth, Dip and Roll values as shown below.

 

To commence the Survey:

The is requires to be calibrated (see Calibrate TN14 on page 1) and attached to the rig (see Attach Instrument on page 1) before commencing.

  1. Begin from the Drillhole screen displaying the Azimuth and Dip compass.

 

 

  1. Position the rig until the planned values align with actual values:
    1. (1) Actual Azimuth with
    2. (2) Planned True North Azimuth or Mine Grid Azimuth
    1. (3) Actual Dip with
    2. (4) Planned Dip

  2. As the TN14 is moved, the app updates displaying the actual azimuth and dip. 

    The actual Azimuth and Dip change from red to green when it is within +/- 0.5° of the planned azimuth and dip.

     

    1. Azimuth is in range, indicated in green.

  1. Dip is in range, indicated in green.

For optimal results, keep the Roll close to 0°.

 

If the Dip reaches 85° to 90° or -85° to -90°, an indicator warning displays.


  1. Select Save once satisfied with result, else Cancel.

Rig Alignment are either green or red, however, the objective is to achieve an accurate (green) value.

Collar Captures are either green or red, as the objective is to capture an existing drillhole. Therefore, it is not about alignment (green values), as the hole trajectory is already set.
